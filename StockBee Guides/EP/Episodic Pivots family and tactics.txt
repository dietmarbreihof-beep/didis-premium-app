Summary of EG’s Discussion on Episodic Pivots Family and Tactics

EG explores the Episodic Pivots (EP) trading setup and its variations, emphasizing the identification of catalysts that drive significant stock moves. He distinguishes between genuine catalysts (e.g., sales growth, turnarounds) and story-driven surges, advocating for tactics like delayed reaction entries to manage risk. The discussion highlights market evolution post-COVID, where stories now sustain multiple legs, and stresses adapting tactics while maintaining core principles. EG critiques misapplications of EP concepts, urging focus on high-volume signatures and plausible stories over chasing every breakout.

Classic EP Section

Classic EPs occur on neglected stocks with game-changing catalysts, such as explosive sales growth (>39% for multiple quarters), big orders, turnarounds, or events like drug approvals/wars/natural disasters. Examples include AMD (OpenAI catalyst), HIMS (Nvidia tie-up), SMCI (earnings raise), MP (rare earth deal), APLD ($15B orders), WULF (crypto-to-AI pivot), ANF/RAIL (turnarounds). Signature traits: highest lifetime volume, prior neglect (sideways/downtrend), and real economic impact, leading to 50-200% moves in days/weeks, scanned via NTRT/MTRT checklists or bucket zero for sustained growth.

EP 9 Million Section

EP 9 million targets story-driven stocks surging on 9M+ volume without real earnings/sales, post-COVID phenomenon where narratives (e.g., AI, quantum, crypto) fuel big moves. Examples: BigBear.ai, Rigetti Computing, QBTS (700% move). Focus on plausible stories, high volume as validation; rarer genuine catalysts but frequent opportunities (300-500/year). Entry via OPG (on-premise gap) or pre-market if volume hits early, aiming for 40-50% velocity moves in days/weeks.

Delayed Reaction EP Section

Delayed Reaction EP (DEP) is an entry tactic, not a separate setup, used on large-cap (>10B) EPs or EP 9 million to avoid chasing gaps (20-40%+). Wait for pullback/consolidation post-catalyst, then enter on breakout for lower risk, tighter stops, larger positions. Examples: AMD (post-OpenAI), Nvidia (post-catalyst), STX/NEXT/MOVE. Ideal for big caps needing time to absorb buying; risk missing straight runners but suits risk-averse trading.

Tactics and Market Observations Section

Tactics evolve: Pre-market for early surges, OPG for simplicity (2.5-5% risk), avoid <5% gaps on large caps. Critique: Mislabeling breakouts/biotechs as EPs leads to low win rates; penny stocks (<$5) need separate pump/dump setups, not EP. Market shift post-COVID: Stories dominate over fundamentals, velocity over duration; adapt or lag. No long-term holds – aim for quick 40-50% gains.
